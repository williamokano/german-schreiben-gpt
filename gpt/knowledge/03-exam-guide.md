# Exam Guide — Writing Modules: TELC, Goethe, DTZ, TestDaF

Reference document for Deutsch Schreibtrainer. Used for: generating original exam-format practice tasks, running Examiner mode, and answering format questions.

**Copyright and accuracy rules (binding):**
- Never reproduce, paraphrase-from-memory, or claim to possess real exam tasks. Every practice task is invented, and says so: "Originalgetreues Übungsformat — keine echte Prüfungsaufgabe."
- Exam providers revise formats. When a student's exam is imminent, recommend once per session that they verify the current format and try the official free Modellsatz on the provider's site (telc.net, goethe.de, bamf.de, testdaf.de).

## Goethe-Zertifikat B1 — Schreiben (most-requested format)

| Teil | Task | Words | Time | Register |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Informal e-mail to a friend (e.g., react to news, invite, apologize), 3 content points | ~80 | 20 min | du |
| 2 | Forum/guest-book post: opinion on a stated topic with reasons and an example | ~80 | 25 min | neutral |
| 3 | Semi-formal e-mail (e.g., to a teacher, course office, colleague): decline, request, apologize — 1 content point, diplomatically | ~40 | 15 min | Sie |

**Rating criteria (apply in Examiner mode):** Erfüllung (all points, adequate length, correct text type), Kohärenz (structure, connectors), Wortschatz (range + accuracy), Strukturen (range + accuracy). Grade each qualitatively (voll erfüllt / überwiegend / teilweise / kaum), then give an overall verdict and the two highest-leverage improvements.

**Task blueprint (generate originals):** Teil 1 — pick a persona friend + life event (moved, new job, party, illness) + 3 points mixing reaction/information/proposal. Teil 2 — pick a debatable everyday topic (Handys in der Schule, Homeoffice, Online-Shopping); require Meinung + Begründung + Beispiel. Teil 3 — pick an authority-lite recipient + a conflict (missing class, rescheduling, a request) requiring polite Konjunktiv II.

## DTZ — Schreiben

One letter/e-mail, 30 minutes, assessed at A2 or B1. Situations are practical-integrative: to a Vermieter (repair, noise), Schule/Kita (excuse, appointment), employer (sick note follow-up, schedule change), Amt/insurance (request, complaint), a course provider (registration, cancellation).

**Structure candidates must produce:** Betreff, Anrede (Sehr geehrte/r …), reason for writing, the 3–4 Leitpunkte in sensible order, polite closing request, Grußformel + name.

**Rating logic (Examiner mode):** (1) Aufgabenbewältigung — all Leitpunkte addressed with adequate development; (2) Kommunikative Gestaltung — text type conventions, register, connectors; (3) Formale Richtigkeit — errors relative to level (B1 requires subordinate clauses attempted and mostly working). Verdict format: "Schreiben: B1 erreicht / A2 erreicht / unter A2", plus rationale.

**Task blueprint:** recipient (Vermieter/Schule/Arbeitgeber/Kursleitung/Amt) × trigger (defect, conflict, absence, request, change) × 3–4 Leitpunkte, always including one that forces a question or request (generates Konjunktiv II) and one with a time reference (generates temporal clauses).

## telc B1/B2 — Schreiben

**B1:** one letter, 4 Leitpunkte, semi-formal or personal, 30 min. All four points must appear and be developed (one sentence each is the floor, two is safe). Criteria: Berücksichtigung der Leitpunkte, kommunikative Gestaltung, formale Richtigkeit. A letter that misses two Leitpunkte fails on content regardless of grammar quality — enforce this in Examiner mode.

**B2:** one letter from a choice of two, 30 min, ~150–200 words. Typical genres: formale Beschwerde, Bewerbungsanschreiben, Leserbrief, Anfrage. Criteria weigh Inhaltsangemessenheit, Textaufbau, Ausdrucksvermögen, Korrektheit. At B2, formulaic Redemittel without development caps the score — require concrete elaboration.

**Blueprint:** genre × counterpart (company, landlord, editor, employer) × concrete trigger with details worth referencing (invoice number, date, product) — supporting material should contain those details so the student must extract and reuse them.

## telc C1 Hochschule — Schriftlicher Ausdruck

70 minutes, one structured text (~350 words) from a choice of two prompts, usually with a graphic or short input text. Expected: Einleitung, strukturierte Argumentation mit Beispielen, begründete eigene Position, Schluss. Academic-neutral register, nominal style welcome but clarity first. In Examiner mode grade: Aufgabenbezug und Inhalt, Textorganisation, Ausdruck (Differenziertheit), Korrektheit.

## Goethe B2 / C1 — Schreiben

**B2 Teil 1** (~150 words, 50 min): forum contribution discussing a societal topic — expected moves: introduce the issue, weigh two sides (concession!), own position with reasons. **Teil 2** (~100 words, 25 min): semi-formal message to a superior/organizer handling an awkward situation (absence, problem report) diplomatically.
**C1 Teil 1** (~230 words): article/forum text developing an argument from prompts, with structured paragraphs and a headline where asked. **Teil 2** (~120 words): formal/semi-formal message. Criteria at both levels: Erfüllung, Kohärenz, Wortschatz, Strukturen — with sharply rising expectations for differentiated vocabulary and complex structures at C1.

## TestDaF — Schriftlicher Ausdruck

One 60-minute task (paper format): describe data from a graph/table (selection, not exhaustive reading), then argue a related question with pros, cons, and a justified position, in academic-neutral register. Digital format splits this into two shorter tasks. Graded TDN 3 / 4 / 5 on: Gesamteindruck (readability, flow), Behandlung der Aufgabe (both parts complete, data accurately used), sprachliche Realisierung (range, accuracy, academic register).

**Blueprint:** invent a plausible dataset (student numbers, media use, commuting, energy mix — 4–6 data points, two time points or categories, one striking development) + a policy-ish question attached to it. Render the data as a markdown table labeled as "Grafik (beschrieben)". Teach the standard moves: Einleitungssatz zur Grafik, 2–3 ausgewählte Daten mit Vergleich, Überleitung, Argumentation, Fazit.

## Examiner Mode — Session Script (all exams)

1. Confirm exam + level, state the format and time budget, present the original task.
2. Optional: offer a real-time timer ("Sag Bescheid, wenn du anfängst — ich sage dir nach der Hälfte der Zeit Bescheid" is not possible; instead: "Schreib ohne Hilfsmittel und stoppe deine Zeit selbst — Richtwert: 30 Minuten.").
3. No help during writing beyond task clarification.
4. Grade per the exam's criteria above — one short paragraph per criterion, quote evidence from the student's text.
5. Verdict in the exam's own vocabulary (points band / TDN / "B1 erreicht").
6. Debrief: the two changes with the highest score impact, then offer targeted practice for exactly those.

**Strictness calibration:** grade like a trained rater on a normal day — not the most lenient, not the harshest. When in doubt between two bands, give the lower one and say what would have secured the higher.
